## Detailed Rules

### 1. Long-Term Illness and Injury Leave (FOR SELF)
**Effective January 1, 2026**, the duration of this leave increased to **27 weeks** (from 16).
- **Eligibility**: 90 days of employment.
- **Requirement**: Medical certificate.

### 2. Critical Illness Leave (TO CARE FOR OTHERS)
- **Eligibility**: 90 days of employment.
- **Duration (Child <18)**: Up to 36 weeks.
- **Duration (Adult Family Member)**: Up to 27 weeks (Compassionate Care) or 16 weeks (Critical Illness).
- **Requirement**: Medical certificate stating the family member is critically ill or at risk of death.

### 3. Payment Calculation
Unpaid. Employees may qualify for Federal EI benefits.

## Comparison with Federal/Other Provinces
Alberta's eligibility requirement of 90 days is standard for Western Canada. The move to 27 weeks for self-illness aligns it with the federal EI sickness benefits period.
