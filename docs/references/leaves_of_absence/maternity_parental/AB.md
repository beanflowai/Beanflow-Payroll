## Detailed Rules

### Eligibility
- Must have been employed by the same employer for at least **90 days**.
- **Maternity**: Pregnant employees.
- **Parental**: Birth mothers, fathers, and adoptive parents.

### Entitlement
- **Maternity**: 16 weeks. Can start 13 weeks before birth. At least 6 weeks must be taken after birth unless medical certificate allows early return.
- **Parental**: Up to 62 weeks. Must be completed within 78 weeks of birth/adoption.
- **Combined**: If the same employee takes both, the total is capped at 78 weeks.

### Payment Calculation
- Unpaid.
- Employers must provide same or equivalent job upon return.

### Notice & Documentation
- **Notice**: Employee must provide at least 6 weeks' written notice.
- **Notice to Return**: Employee must provide at least 4 weeks' written notice before returning.
- **Documentation**: Medical certificate for maternity leave; proof of adoption for parental leave.

## Comparison with Other Jurisdictions
- Alberta's 90-day eligibility is standard for many provinces, whereas BC and Federal have no minimum.
- Combined 78 weeks aligns with BC and Federal.
